### Personal & Institutional Details

* Name: Ahmedin Jebel (also spelled Ahmedin Jebel, Ahmedin Jubel, etc) — in Amharic: አሕመዲን ጀበል.
* Nationality: Ethiopian.
* Religious Role: He is referred to as “Ustaz” (teacher/preacher), indicating his role as an Islamic educator and public speaker.
* Professional / Social Roles: preacher, community leader, historian/author, activist.
* Institutional affiliation: He has served as **Social Affairs Advisor** to the Ethiopian Islamic Affairs Supreme Council (EIASC). ([MDPI][1])

---

### Key Life & Career Events

* **Muslim Arbitration Committee (MAC)**: In 2012, Ahmedin Jebel was one of 17 Muslim leaders selected to the Muslim Arbitration Committee which was formed to address grievances of the Ethiopian Muslim community concerning governmental and religious-institution interference. ([MDPI][1])
* **Arrest & Imprisonment**: In 2012 he was arrested in connection with the MAC leadership along with other defendants, facing charges including terrorism, conspiracy to establish an Islamic state, and public incitement. ([Horn Affairs][2])

  * On August 3, 2015, a court sentenced him to 22 years in prison. ([Addis Standard][3])
  * He was released on February 14, 2018. ([Federal Newswire][4])
* **Public Advocacy & Speeches**:

  * On June 3 2023 he delivered an impassioned speech at a funeral for Muslims killed in protests over mosque demolitions in Addis Ababa. In the speech he warned the community about being used by others, and called for unity, strategy and moving forward. ([MDPI][1])
  * He has publicly addressed the demolition of mosques and other community grievances, e.g., commenting on mosque-demolitions and force used by security forces. ([S2Jnews][5])
* **Political Candidacy and Withdrawal**: In the 2021 national election cycle he ran as a candidate for the House of People’s Representatives for Jimma city but later withdrew his candidacy citing personal reasons, though votes were still cast for him. ([Addis Standard][6])

### Publications & Research

* He authored a book titled *ኢትዮጵያውያን ሙስሊሞች ከ 615-1700 የጭቆናና የትግል ታሪክ* (“Ethiopian Muslims: History of Persecution and Struggle from 615-1700”) published 2011 (Amharic, 272 pages). ([LibraryCat][7])
* According to academic sources, he wrote **three books** concerning the history of Ethiopian Muslims and their relationship with Christian kings, and **two other books** on Islam/contemporary issues. ([MDPI][1])
